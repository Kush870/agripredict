🌍 Real-World Problem: Food Security & Agricultural Efficiency

By 2050, the world needs to feed 10 billion people. Yet most smallholder farmers in countries like India have no access to data-driven tools. AgriPredict uses machine learning to predict crop yield before planting — helping farmers optimise resources, reduce waste, and maximise output.

What Problem Does This Solve?

  • Farmers struggle to predict yield without expensive lab testing
  • Overuse of fertilizers harms soil and adds unnecessary cost
  • Climate variability makes traditional knowledge unreliable
  • Government food planning needs accurate crop forecasts

AgriPredict uses 9 input variables to predict yield in seconds — a task that would otherwise require agronomists and weeks of analysis.

How the Model Works

Algorithm: XGBoost Regressor
XGBoost (Extreme Gradient Boosting) builds decision trees sequentially, where each tree corrects the errors of the previous ones. This iterative approach often achieves higher accuracy than Random Forest for tabular data.

  • 100 estimators (trees), max depth 20
  • Trained on 800K samples, tested on 200K
  • R² = 0.91 (excellent accuracy)
  • MAE = 0.41 tons/hectare
